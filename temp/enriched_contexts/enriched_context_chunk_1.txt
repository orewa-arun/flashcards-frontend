## DEFINITIONS (Use for `concise` answers)
- **Purchase Stage**: This stage encompasses all customer interactions with the brand during the purchase. It includes behaviors such as choice, ordering, and payment. It is considered as the most “temporally compressed” of the three stages.
- **Partner-owned touch points**: These touch points are jointly designed, controlled and managed by the firm and its partner(s). The partners could be multivendor partners, multichannel distributors partners, communication partners, etc.
- **Social/external touch points**: These touch points are all external people that may influence the customer experience. It may concerns the advices of customers (e.g. Comments on TripAdvisor) or peer influences, independent information sources, etc. Sometimes influencers may be independent and sometimes they are in line with firm expectations.
- **Post purchase Stage**: The last stage concerns all interactions with the brand and its environment after purchase. It includes the use/consumption of the product/service, and service requests. It is an important phase where the customer will evaluate his shopping experience. Based on this evaluation, he will engage or not with the brand, become loyal or not, use negative or positive W.O.M’s.
- **Brand-owned touch points**: These touch points customer interactions are designed, controlled and managed by the firm. It may concern the marketing mix (products, price strategy, service, distribution) and more broadly the brand owned media such as advertising, websites, loyalty programs, etc.
- **Customer-owned touch points**: These touch points concerned all customer actions that are part of the customer experience. The company, its partners or others do not influence or control these actions. These actions may include customers reflecting on their needs and desires and the transformation of a company product into a use not intended by the firm.
- **Pre purchase Stage**: This stage encompasses all direct or indirect customer interactions with the brand before the purchase. It generally starts with the “need/goal/impulse recognition to consideration of satisfying that need/goal/impulse with a purchase” including the search stage (Lemon & Verhoef, 2016, p.76).

## CONCEPT RELATIONSHIPS (Use for `common_mistakes`)
These distinctions help prevent confusion:
- 'Brand-Owned' vs 'Customer-Owned' - Don't confuse these related but distinct concepts
- 'Brand-Owned' vs 'Partner-Owned' - Don't confuse these related but distinct concepts
- 'Partner-Owned' vs 'Customer-Owned' - Don't confuse these related but distinct concepts
- 'Purchase' vs 'Consumption' - Don't confuse these related but distinct concepts
- The stages are sequential - understand the order and what happens at each
